Project: "EYP Guidance Counselling"

“EYP Guidance Counselling” is a media project born from my own journey as a first-time Official/Media Team Member. I once believed that Editors were normal Media Team Members solely designated for editing photos. This misunderstanding, along with others stemming from my unfamiliarity with the EYP process, sparked the creation of this project. During the Collaborative Digital Training, this project aimed to foster a comfortable space where Trainees could openly discuss advancing their EYP careers with Trainers. Held on the training’s final day, the counseling session’s insights are revisited and expanded upon in this post. I hope you find these tips helpful as you embark on your rewarding EYP journey!

Project: "Training in Memes"

In this project, which is meant to be a casual break from the otherwise highly theoretical training, participants (Trainers & Trainees) were asked to make and share memes on their spontaneous thoughts and feelings about the training.

Project: "Interpreting history through art"

Adhering to Basel’s rich art culture and addressing the AFET committee’s topic of colonialism, the goal of this academic project is to allow participants the freedom to process and express their thoughts and emotions on the complex issue by making art - the language without words. Following the session’s paperless policy, participants were asked to make a small clay artwork, which can be shaped and reshaped reflecting the versatility and maturation of their author, or reused for a different purpose. The project invites participants and viewers alike to ponder critically about their own and their society’s relationship with colonialism - the first step of making social change.

Project: "1,2,3, breathe!"

With its constant energisers, games and debates, EYP is a unique environment where the energy levels always stay high. This project aims to explore and showcase how EYPers of different personalities relax and adapt to EYP’s high energy nature. It is precisely the occasional breaks that are the thing allowing us as EYPers to contribute to the excitement within the session, thus spreading positivity in the organisation as a whole.

Project: "The Tale of the Éislek NSC"

I started the project looking to make something fun and slightly ridiculous. Inspired by the old castles of Luxembourg, I decided to retell the session with a fairy tale spin. A random conversation on the bus led to my discovering the wooden Frog Princess statue in front of the castle of Wiltz, the Committee Work venue of Éislek 2024. This project is (very loosely) based on the fairy tale. I was also prompted to use Powerpoint again for the first time in years, after seeing a reel demonstrating the potentials of the Morph transition. Using something seen as basic like Powerpoint to make an entire animation was an incredibly rewarding experience. The result of these several little things is what you are seeing now. Enjoy <3
